Frequently Asked Questions

Who is Dr. Krista Li?

Krista holds a Ph.D in History from the University of New Brunswick. She has taught at various Canadian universities, including the University of Alberta, Grant MacEwan University and the University of New Brunswick. Her undergraudate work featured a minor in English Literature focusing on Shakesperian drama. She has been tutoring privately since 2009. Fluent in English and Mandarin Chinese, Krista drives her students to reach their true potential.

What can I get help with?

At Phoenix, we specialize in humanities tutoring. We offer sessions in English Language Arts, Social Studies, the fundamentals of writing, how to conduct research, and essay support for both high school and university level classes. We also offer support for those wishing to improve their English abilities through conversation and those looking to prepare for the IELTS exam.

How do I get a tutor?

Call us at 780-756-8781 or email at to set up an appointment. We will be happy to work with you to find a time to book a consultation.

Can I work with the same tutor again?

Yes! All sessions are taught by Dr. Krista Li. Working with the same tutor allows you to establish a raport and allows Krista to get to know your specific abilities and learning style. This makes for a customized and personalized experience that you will not find elsewhere.

Will you come to my home to tutor?

Unfotunately not. All classes take place at Krista's home classroom, where she can easily access resources and material.

What's the minimum time to work with a tutor?

Sessions are a minimum of one hour. That gives you time to discuss the issues at hand, complete exercises, and go over work that will be assigned for the next session or evaluate work completed from the last session.

About Me

Krista L. Li

Krista Li holds a Ph.D in History from the University of New Brunswick. Her undergraduate degree, along with her Master of Arts, was completed at Memorial University of Newfoundland.


She completed a minor in English Literature, with an emphasis in Shakespearean tragedy. Since 2009, Krista has worked as an instructor at the Department of History and Classics at the University of Alberta, teaching topics in post-Confederation Canadian and modern Chinese history.


She has also taught at the University of New Brunswick and Grant MacEwan University. Although she is a native speaker of English, Krista also speaks Mandarin Chinese fluently and has a love of Chinese culture and history. She is a highly sought after private tutor and knows what it takes to unlock the potential of each child that she works with. She has been tutoring in Edmonton since 2009. In her spare time, she enjoys Chinese traditional dance, reading, and embarking on new adventures!
